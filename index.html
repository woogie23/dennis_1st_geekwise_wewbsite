My Animal Friends!

CHEETAH

CHEETAH, POWERFUL HUNTERS!

Cheetah are solitary animals. Males and females are only seen together for mating purposes. The male is a solitary hunter, as is the female, but the female will hunt with her cubs to teach them. They are the only large cat whose claws do not retract. Because they are solitary hunters, they are subject to having their food stolen by packs of other animals, so they will not fight other predators for their prey, so as to not be injured and not be able to hunt,which coul lead to their starvation.

ELEPHANTS

ELEPHANT PROTECTING!

Elephants are structured in a matriarchal society, with the elder female usually in charge of the herd. Several generations will live together with all the females helping to raise and protect the young. Adult males are only around for breeding purposes or are juveniles who will leave the herd when three to five years of age. Juveniles males will often travel in small bands until they attempt to attract their own breeding herds, and then they will often live solitary lives.
